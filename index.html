Labour Force Participation Rate Trends By Continent

From the stacked bar chart, it is obvious to say that Asia and Europe consistently have the highest rates, followed by Africa and the Americas. A notable observation is the relative stability across all continents over the years, indicating consistent workforce engagement globally.


However, subtle fluctuations, particularly a dip in 2020, could suggest the impact of significant global events like the COVID-19 pandemic on labor markets. This trend allows us to compare workforce dynamics and understand how different regions responded to economic shifts.


Labor Force Growth By Continent

Asia shows a steady increase in its labor force, leading globally, while Africa also demonstrates notable growth, potentially indicating expanding employment opportunities and economic activity. On the other hand, the Americas, Europe, and Oceania show more modest growth, with Oceania remaining relatively flat, reflecting its smaller population.


The contrasting rates of growth provide insight into the economic development of these regions, with Africa and Asia leading in terms of labor force expansion. The flat trend in Oceania highlights its comparatively smaller contribution to the global labor pool.


Unemployment Rate Shifts By Continent

Oceania consistently ranks first in having the lowest unemployment rates, while the Americas fluctuate and eventually rank highest post-2020.


Asia and Europe swap ranks a few times, showing some variability in their relative positions, while Africa remains relatively stable, maintaining a mid-range unemployment rate rank.


The year 2020 has drastically affected unemployment rates, likely due to the global pandemic.


Working Poverty Rate Shifts By Continent

In 2017, Asia sharply rose from 3rd to 1st in working poverty, indicating worsening conditions. Since then, Asia has maintained the highest poverty rate. Meanwhile, Africa dropped from 1st to 2nd, suggesting slight improvement compared to Asia. Europe and the Americas remained stable, with Europe consistently having the lowest rate and the Americas fluctuating between 2nd and 3rd.


2017 marked a key shift: Asia's poverty worsened, while Africa improved slightly, with Europe and the Americas maintaining stable rates.
